Week 5

Daily Standups:

26th May 2025

What did I do today?

I finally finished my typography poster. After a week of being completely stuck, the idea just clicked. I’d spent the weekend experimenting with different directions after feeling like I couldn’t code the original concept. I reached a point where I wanted to scrap the original idea completely and start fresh. However, on Monday morning, something fell into place and the solution worked. Although this blog is meant to focus on the main project and not the Type Brief, I’ve talked about both throughout, so it feels worth noting that the poster is now complete and off my plate.

Were there any blockers?

We have a large job happening at work at the moment, an annual agricultural expo, that’s taken a lot of time and energy. It’s been difficult to balance that with my school workload, and I expect it to continue being a blocker throughout the week.

Retrospective:

What went well?

  • • I finished my typography poster after feeling stuck for over a week, which was a big relief. The breakthrough came after experimenting with new ideas, and it helped restore some momentum.
  • • I refined my brand direction based on lecturer feedback, including researching similar apps and reworking my logo and fonts.
  • • I started building my HiFi prototype and my brand guidelines.
  • • I managed to juggle feedback, revisions, and new tasks fairly well, despite some major distractions during the week.

    • What didn’t go well?

    • • Juggling full-time work, uni, and personal commitments was extremely difficult this week. A large job at work, a graduation dinner, and a visiting friend all made it harder to focus.
    • • I didn’t feel like I was able to give uni as much attention as I would’ve liked, which added to my stress.
    • • I’ve been struggling with self-doubt… Wondering if my work is actually any good, or just average. In an oversaturated industry, feeling average can be incredibly discouraging.

    What can be improved?

    • • Find ways to give my schoolwork the attention it deserves, even when work and life are demanding.
    • • Create a bit more structure around personal projects so I don’t lose traction when life gets in the way.
    • • Try to remind myself that feeling uncertain or uncomfortable doesn’t mean I’m failing, it often just means I’m growing.

    What will I try next week?

    • • Keep pushing forward even when things feel unclear or hard, reminding myself that the discomfort is part of the process.
    • • Reflect on the feedback I got from Simon about how we’re being fast-tracked for industry, and try to trust that the pressure and growing pains will pay off.
    • • Be honest with myself about where I’m at emotionally and mentally, and allow space for those feelings without letting them derail me.
